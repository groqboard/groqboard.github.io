Getting Started

Up and Running in Minutes

Four simple steps to transform how you type on Android.

1

Enable GroqBoard

Open your Android keyboard settings and enable GroqBoard as an input method. Just takes a few taps.

2

Add Your Groq API Key

Get your free API key at console.groq.com and paste it into the settings. Simple signup, no credit card needed.

3

Personalize & Train

Optional but powerful. Use Custom Instructions to teach GroqBoard your writing style and names. Import a .txt file via Train Predictions to learn your vocabulary. WhatsApp exports work great.

4

Start Speaking!

Open any app, switch to GroqBoard, tap the mic button, and watch your voice become text instantly.

FAQ

Good to Know

How much does GroqBoard cost?

GroqBoard is free to try for 7 days — no credit card required. After the trial, unlock a lifetime license with a one-time payment (~$4.99 USD). No subscription, ever. Google Play adjusts the price to your local currency automatically.

What if I try it and don't like it?

The 7-day free trial lets you test everything before paying — no risk at all. If you unlock the lifetime license and change your mind, Google Play gives you a refund window. Just open the app's page on the Play Store, tap Refund, and you're done.

I already purchased GroqBoard before v5.0 — do I need to pay again?

No! If you purchased GroqBoard before v5.0, your lifetime license is activated automatically — no action needed. If it doesn't appear, you can request an activation code by emailing support@groqboard.com.

Why is GroqBoard so much cheaper than other AI keyboard apps?

Most AI keyboard apps charge a monthly subscription because they pay for every transcription out of their own pocket — API costs add up fast. GroqBoard works differently: you connect your own free Groq API key, and your audio goes directly from your phone to Groq's servers. GroqBoard never pays for your usage, which means no per-user cost and no reason to charge a subscription. You get the same state-of-the-art AI for a one-time $4.99 instead of $10–30/month.

What AI model does it use?

Voice recognition runs on Whisper v3 Large, one of the most accurate speech-to-text models available, running on Groq's ultra-fast hardware. Smart predictions use a LLaMA model, also via Groq. Both are state-of-the-art open models.

Can I make it learn my writing style?

Yes. You can import a WhatsApp chat export and the app trains on your own messages, learning your vocabulary, phrases, and preferred language. You can also add custom words manually. The more you use it, the smarter it gets.

Does it need internet to work?

Voice recognition and AI predictions require an internet connection; they run on Groq's servers. The full QWERTY keyboard and swipe typing work completely offline, so you can still type without a signal.

Power User Tips

Unlock GroqBoard's full potential with these advanced configurations.

⚙

Quick Settings Tile: Switch to GroqBoard Instantly

Android lets you add a Quick Settings tile to switch keyboards with one tap — no more digging through settings. To enable it, connect your phone to a computer and run this ADB command:

adb shell cmd statusbar add-tile com.alegol.groqboard/.KeyboardTileService

Once added, you'll see a GroqBoard tile in your notification shade. Tap it to instantly switch your active keyboard to GroqBoard.

Pro tip: Move it to the top

  • Swipe down to expand your notification shade
  • Tap the pencil/edit icon to rearrange tiles
  • Drag the GroqBoard tile to the first row so it's always visible without fully expanding

Now switching to GroqBoard is literally one swipe and one tap away.

🎧

Hands-Free Voice Input with a Physical Button

Want to start and stop voice dictation without touching the screen? GroqBoard supports external triggers — meaning you can map any physical button (volume key, Bluetooth remote, headset button) to toggle transcription on and off.

To set this up, use an automation app like:

  • MacroDroid (free, beginner-friendly)
  • Tasker (paid, very powerful)
  • Automate (free, flowchart-based)

The general setup is:

  • Trigger: Choose a physical button press (e.g., volume down, a Bluetooth button, or headset button)
  • Action: Send a broadcast intent to toggle GroqBoard's voice input:
Intent action: com.alegol.groqboard.TOGGLE_VOICE Package: com.alegol.groqboard

That's it. Press the button once to start listening, press again to stop and send the transcribed text to whatever app you're in.

Use cases:

  • Dictate messages while driving with a Bluetooth steering wheel button
  • Quick voice notes with a press of the volume key while your phone is in your pocket
  • Hands-free transcription at your desk using a cheap Bluetooth remote
